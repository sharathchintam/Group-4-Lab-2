About Kerala

Kerala is a small state lying to the south most part of India. It is covered by Arabian sea in the east and Western Ghats in the west. Kerala is famous for ecotourism and backwaters. Even though it is a part of India like every other state Kerala has its own culture and history. Kerala is known as God's own country since it is believed that it is created directly by Parashurama an Avatar of the Hindu god lord Vishnu.

In Kerala there are many festivals like Onam, Vishu, Thiruvathira, Thrissur Pooram, and Mahanavami, Onam being the main festival. Kerala is one of the states in India where Hindu, Islam and Christians are equally populated, so in Kerala can see centuries old temples, mosques and churches.

One of the main attractions of Kerala is its art forms showcasing the cultural value of Kerala such as Kathakali, Chakyarkoothu, Ottamthullal, Theyam, Mohiniyattam and Pulikkali. When it comes to main sports and games of Kerala the important ones are Kabbadi, Aranmula vallam kali and kalaripayattu(Kalari).

1. Festivals of Kerala

1.1 Onam


Onam feast in Plantain leaf                                                      A floral design

Onam is one of the main festival of Kerala and it falls in 22nd nakshathra Thiruvonam in malayalam calendar. It is believed to be the visiting time of the King Mahabali to visit his people. Onam comes along the time of harvesting and Onam feast typically consist of fresh harvested rice with many curries spread in plantain leaf accompanied by payasam. a dessert made with rice flakes. Onam is a colourful festival and during the time of Onam people put pookalam(a floral design usually done in ground or mud floor) in front of their house.

1.2 Vishu


Vishukani

Vishu festival indicates the starting of the spring season. Vishu is celebrated in Kerala on the first day of the Medam or Malayalam month which marks the starting of the harvest season. Malayalis(people of Kerala) all over the world dedicate this day to the Hindu god Lord Vishnu. A VishuKani is prepared a day before the Vishu festival. It is the symbol of prosperity and good luck.The term 'VishuKani' means the very first thing to be seen at the dawn of Vishu and it is believed that by doing this the New Year brings prosperity in the life of the devotee.

1.3 Thrissur Pooram


A mass of people enjoying umberlla exchange in Thrissur Pooram

Thrissur Pooram was an idea of Raja Rama Varma (Sakthan Thampuran), the Maharaja of Cochin (1790–1805).It is one of the most crowded temple festival in Kerala. Initially it was a small festival confined to a single temple and it was known by the name Arattupuzha pooram but later it became a big festival by combining all the temples around the district of Thrissur. Among the 10 temples, the most important temple is Vadukkunathan temple. It is a temple dedicated under the name of Lord Shiva. List of other temples include:

  1. Thiruvambadi Sri Krishna Temple
  2. Kanimangalam Sastha Temple
  3. Laloor Bhagavathy Temple
  4. Ayyanthole Karthyayani Temple
  5. Nethilakkavu Baghavathy Temple

The main high light of Thrissur Pooram is the hundreds of elephants with decorative ornaments and the golden umbrella exchange is another attraction of Pooram. Lakhs of people all around the world come to enjoy Thrissur Pooram from all around the world.
